Butter Chicken

Creamy sauce, so rich,
        Tender chicken, spice and warmth,
        Flavors dance with bliss.

INGREDIENTS

  • 500g chicken breast or thighs, boneless and cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup yogurt (optional, for extra creaminess)
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (coriander leaves) for garnish

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Marinate the chicken (optional)
    In a bowl, combine the chicken cubes with 1 tablespoon of yogurt, a pinch of salt, and 1 teaspoon of garam masala. Let it marinate for about 20-30 minutes (or even overnight in the fridge if you have time).
  2. Cook the chicken
    Heat 1 tablespoon of butter in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook until they are lightly browned and cooked through, about 7-8 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pan and set aside.
  3. Make the sauce:
    • In the same pan, add another tablespoon of butter. Once melted, add the chopped onion and cook until soft and golden, about 5 minutes.
    • Add the minced garlic and ginger, and cook for 1 more minute until fragrant.
    • Stir in the crushed tomatoes, garam masala, cumin, chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, and salt. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to combine and the sauce to thicken.
  4. Add Cream:
    Lower the heat and stir in the heavy cream and yogurt (if using). Mix well, and let the sauce simmer for 5-6 minutes, allowing it to become creamy and rich.
  5. Combine the chicken and the sauce
    Add the cooked chicken back into the sauce and simmer for another 5 minutes, allowing the chicken to absorb the sauce and become tender.
  6. Garnish and serve
    Garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve your butter chicken with steamed rice or naan bread.
